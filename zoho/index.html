Data Entry Rules

No Duplicate Leads

+
Before creating a new lead, always search the CRM for the contact's name, email, or company. Duplicates pollute reporting and create confusion over ownership. If a duplicate is found, update the existing record instead.

Phone Number Format

+
Always add the country code prefix to every phone number. Format: +[Country Code] [Number]. Example: +91 98765 43210 for India. This ensures calls can be made directly from the CRM and is critical for international teams.

Proper Naming Format

+
Use Title Case for contact names (e.g., "Anand Gupta" not "anand gupta" or "ANAND GUPTA"). For company names, use the official registered name. Consistent formatting makes search and reporting more reliable.

No Fake or Placeholder Data

+
Never enter test data, fake numbers, or placeholder emails (like test@test.com) in the live CRM. Use the sandbox environment for testing. Every record in the CRM should represent a real business contact or opportunity.

Always Update Notes

+
After every interaction ‚Äî call, email, or meeting ‚Äî add a note to the relevant Lead or Deal record. Notes should be concise but informative: what was discussed, key takeaways, and agreed next steps. This is critical for team handovers.

Best Practices
Follow these guidelines consistently to ensure data quality, operational efficiency, and team alignment across the CRM.
01

Never Leave Fields Empty

Every field in the lead and deal form has a purpose. Missing fields create blind spots in your reports and make handovers harder for your team. If you genuinely don't know a value, use "Unknown" as a placeholder rather than leaving it blank.

02

Always Mention Lead Source

The lead source field helps leadership understand which channels are generating the most valuable business. Options include: Referral, Cold Outreach, LinkedIn, Event, Website, Partner, etc. This is required on every new lead.

03

Add Notes After Every Call

Notes should be written within 15 minutes of a call while the conversation is fresh. Include: what was discussed, prospect's key concerns, what was agreed, and what the next action is. Good notes make team handovers seamless.

04

Update Status Regularly

Lead and deal statuses should reflect the current reality at all times. Don't batch-update at end of week ‚Äî update the moment a stage change occurs. Real-time status is what makes the pipeline trustworthy for leadership.

05

Select the Correct Business Vertical

With multiple business lines (Mondee, Miraee, Aarna, Pranik), correct vertical tagging is essential. A misclassified lead gets assigned to the wrong team, causing delays and lost opportunities. Double-check before saving.

06

Respond to Automated Reminders

The CRM sends automated follow-up reminders. These exist for a reason ‚Äî treat them as hard deadlines. Consistently ignoring reminders leads to stale leads and CRM workflows breaking down. Acknowledge and act on every reminder.
